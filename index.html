Avoidance Doesn't
Make You Better

Every chess player has positions they dread. For me, it was the Sicilian. I'd avoid it, play something passive, never actually learn.

What I needed was to face it repeatedly with real-time feedback not post-game analysis where I'd already internalized the wrong move, but in the moment, seeing the engine's suggested line while I'm still feeling the pressure.

That's deliberate practice. That's exposure therapy.

⚠ This tool is designed for unrated and casual training games. Use during rated play violates fair play policies.

Real-Time Engine Overlay

See the engine's suggested line as you're deciding — not after the damage is done. Stay in the pressure zone and learn from it.

🎯

Position Targeting

Select any feared opening or middlegame structure. CiPHER steers you into it repeatedly until the position becomes comfortable.

🧠

Exposure Training Mode

Systematic desensitisation to positions that spike your anxiety. Face them enough times and fear transforms into pattern recognition.

📊

In-Game Feedback

Immediate evaluation of every candidate move. Understand why a line fails while your intuition still remembers choosing it.

🖥️

Cross-Platform

Desktop apps for Windows, macOS and Linux. Native Android app. Browser extension for playing directly on chess sites.

🔒

Training-Only Mode

Cipher is designed to comply with fair-use policy and is not intended for rated or competitive play. It functions strictly as a training tool and is architecturally built with ethical constraints, not as a cheat engine.
